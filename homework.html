Departments

Computer Science Engineering

Computer Science Engineering (CSE) is a dynamic field that combines the principles of computer science and engineering to design, develop, and maintain software systems. CSE professionals work on a wide range of applications, from software development and artificial intelligence to cybersecurity and data science. The curriculum typically includes programming languages, algorithms, data structures, computer architecture, and software engineering. CSE graduates are in high demand across various industries, including technology, finance, healthcare, and entertainment.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical Engineering is a branch of engineering that focuses on the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. Electrical engineers design and develop electrical systems, including power generation and distribution, communication systems, control systems, and electronic devices. The curriculum typically includes courses in circuit analysis, digital logic design, signal processing, and power systems. Electrical engineers play a crucial role in shaping the modern world by contributing to advancements in technology and infrastructure.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering is a versatile discipline that focuses on the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. It encompasses a wide range of applications, including automotive engineering, aerospace engineering, robotics, and energy systems. Mechanical engineers use principles of physics and materials science to create innovative solutions for real-world problems. The curriculum typically includes courses in thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, mechanics of materials, and machine design. Mechanical engineers play a crucial role in shaping the future of technology and industry.
